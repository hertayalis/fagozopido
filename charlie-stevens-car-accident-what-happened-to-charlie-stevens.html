Charlie Stevens Car Accident: What Happened to Charlie Stevens?

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Monday, July 1, 2024

Around 9 p.m. on Friday, Charlie Stevens, 18, was allegedly hit by a car on Beach Road in Goolwa Beach, which is located about 84 km south of Adelaide.

Charlie Stevens, of Colonel Light Gardens, passed away on Saturday at 7:01 p.m. at Flinders Medical Centre after suffering severe injuries.

About nine o’clock on Friday night, Charlie Stevens, eighteen, was reportedly struck by Dhirren Randhawa, also eighteen, in Goolwa, south of Adelaide, while he and his friends were waiting for a bus to go to the Victor Harbor Schoolies celebrations.

Charlie Stevens passed away in the hospital 22 hours later, and Mr. Randhawa was taken into custody moments later on a nearby street after he was thought to have fled the scene.

Charlie Stevens Car Accident

The youngest son of Police Commissioner Grant Stevens of South Australia, Charlie Stevens, was tragically killed in a hit-and-run accident. On November 17, 2023, at around nine o’clock at night, the eighteen-year-old was allegedly hit by a car while attending an end-of-school celebration at Goolwa Beach. Around 67 kilometers south of Adelaide, on Beach Road, the incident took place.

An Encounter Bay man, 18, who was accused of being the hit-and-run driver, was first charged in relation to the incident. The SA Police have since upgraded these charges to include leaving the scene of an accident after causing death, aggravated driving without due care, and failing to provide a thorough response to inquiries. The defendant is scheduled to appear in Christies Beach Magistrates Court after being denied bail.

With an irreversible brain injury, Charlie Stevens was airlifted to Flinders Medical Centre, where he died on Saturday, November 18, at 7:01 p.m., surrounded by loved ones. In a statement, the family thanked SAPOL personnel, first responders, emergency services personnel, and the larger community for their support during this trying time.

Charlie’s mother posted a heartfelt message on social media, honoring her “beautiful boy” who will always be 18 years old. Commissioner Grant Stevens is taking an indefinite leave of absence to spend time with his family. Flowers have been left at the scene of the tragic accident as a token of the community’s deep sympathy for the Stevens family. Beach Road is still closed while investigations are still underway, and major crash investigators have inspected the scene.
