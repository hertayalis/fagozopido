G-Eazy shades ex-girlfriend Halsey in new song after debuting romance with Ashley Benson The US

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Sunday, August 25, 2024

G-EAZY may have gone public with new girlfriend Ashley Benson but he still has ex Halsey on his mind, as he appeared to take aim at the singer on a new album track, calling her a "crazy-a** ex."

The pair's relationship ended in October 2018 after being on-and-off for much of the year, but now the rapper has seemingly thrown shade at Halsey on his track Not Enough.

As well as singing about a “crazy-a** ex” who “would go on TV” and “go on Ellen on me," he also raps: "You dragged my name, I don’t say s**t, but let somebody say ‘G-Eazy,’ you go apes**t."

The 31-year-old may have been referencing a moment earlier in 2020 when she stopped a concert to slam a troll who called out her ex-boyfriend's name.

She screamed into the mic: "Who was it? It’s you? Who said G-Eazy? Who’s f**king yelling it? 'Cause I’ll kick your a** out of the f**King club.

"You won’t disrespect me like that at my own show."

Later the 25-year-old commented on the altercation, writing on social media: "Don’t ever let someone make u feel crazy or unhinged cause ur a woman standing up for urself.

"Don’t tolerate disrespect in the name of being ‘nice.’ Love u."

Fans also believe her single You Should Be Sad was a diss track aimed at her ex.

Elsewhere on the album, the rapper - real name Gerard Gillum - boasted about having the "best sex" he's ever had as he included new girlfriend Ashley Benson on an intimate track.

A phone call between him and Ashley appears at the beginning of the track All The Things You're Searching For, before the actress also sings over the chorus towards the ends.

Ashley and G-Eazy went public just days after her split in early May with British model and actress Cara Delevingne, whom she had been dating since May 2018.

People reported that Ashley and G-Eazy's romance "feels like just a fling for now", with an insider adding: "She's getting over a breakup."
