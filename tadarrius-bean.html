Meet Tadarrius Bean, the Memphis Police Department Officer Who Killed Tyre Nichols

Tadarrius Bean (born on September 18, 1998, Age: 24 years old) is a former Memphis Police Officer who served in MPD (Memphis Police Department) for two years: from 2021 to 2023. He is currently facing charges of killing a 29-year-old innocent guy named Tyre Nichols.

Besides Tadarrius, four more cops who got fired for murdering Nichols are Demetrius Haley, Justin Smith, Emmitt Martin III, and Desmond Mills Jr.

On January 10, the news of police brutality came out, and Tyre’s family and other protestors started demanding the release of the bodycam footage, which Memphis Police released later after conducting an internal investigation. Seeing how brutally Memphis officers behaved, people compare this case with Rodney King and the LAPD.

Bodycam Footage of Tyre Nichols

After the constant demand of Tyre’s family, police released the bodycam footage, which can easily make a tender-hearted person cry. In the video, Tadarrius and his uniformed goons can be seen approaching Nichols’s blue Nissan holding guns in hand.

As the video progresses, the young motorist can be clearly seen cooperating, but the unprofessional police officers kept using abusive language and unnecessary power.

When Ty ran to save his life, he was chased, tased, and sprayed with pepper. In the clip, you can also see a cop kicking Nichol, which was not required at that time as he was lying on the ground.

The Shelby County District Attorney addressing the community amid growing pressure to hold the former officers accountable, said, “While each of the five individuals played a different role in the incident in question, the actions of all of them resulted in the death of Tyre Nichols, and they are all responsible.”

All five officers were booked into jail, each charged with second-degree murder, among other charges. Horrific incidents like these remind us why there is a dire need for police demilitarization.

How & Why Tadarrius Bean With His Fellow Officers Murdered Tyre Nichols?

On the evening of January 7, the young motorist Tyre was driving his car when police officers stopped him. According to sources, he was driving rashly but was sober, but police behaved with him so rudely that he was forced to believe that the men he was dealing with were members of a local gang wearing police uniforms.

Nichols somehow managed to flee from the situation but was later caught by cops who constantly beat him. There is visual proof that confirms that Tadarrius and his fellow law enforcers used undue force while arresting Tyre, which caused multiple injuries. Three days later, on January 10, 2023, Nichols left this world, and his name got added to the long list of victims of police brutality.

From Where is Tadarrius Bean?

Tadarrius Bean (age 24) was born on September 18, 1998, in Olive Branch, Mississippi, United States. He attended Center Hill High School, where he was part of the soccer team. Bean started working when he was 16. His first part-time job was at The Wendy’s Company.

Following school, he moved to Memphis, where he joined the University of Mississippi to study criminal justice and law enforcement. In August 2019, he interned at UoM’s Mississippi Police Department and two years later joined the service as a police officer.

His Twitter account is still current, but he has not posted anything for a long time. What Tadarrius did with the Nichols was horrible, and hopefully, justice will be served.
